Our story

We didn't plan
any of this.

A math degree, a layoff, a metal band, and a cross-country move later — here we are, building software for the people who actually keep things running.

How we got here

The unplanned path

It started with a metal band. Saebyl and Stephen met when they both joined the same group — they'd known each other for about a year before things clicked — and were both subsequently kicked out of it. Best thing that ever happened to us.

One month after they started dating, Saebyl was laid off. Not the ideal start to a relationship. But Stephen didn't flinch, and Saebyl did what any panicking math-degree-holder does: sent out a hundred applications and hoped for the best.

One of those applications landed at Amazon. Not because of a master plan — because of sheer volume and a little luck. They packed up and relocated from Oklahoma to Redmond, Washington. Stephen traded the trades for the Pacific Northwest. Saebyl traded financial services for one of the biggest data organizations in the world.

Years at Amazon as a Business Intelligence Engineer gave Saebyl something unexpected: a front-row seat to what enterprise-grade data infrastructure actually looks like — and a growing frustration that none of it was accessible to the small businesses back home in Oklahoma. The ones run by people like Stephen's family. Like their friends. Like the contractors and restaurant owners and tradespeople who built everything around them.

Meanwhile Stephen was doing what he's always done — showing up, working hard, learning every trade that crossed his path. Restaurants, bars, construction, wind turbines, electrical work. The kind of career that lives in your hands and your back. And at 37, with a lifetime of domain knowledge that no classroom could replicate, the question became: what's next?

The answer was Finding The Beat LLC. Not a pivot. Not a side hustle. A decision to build something real, together, for the people they actually know. They're moving back to Oklahoma. They're bringing everything they've learned. And they're done watching great small businesses get left behind by software that was never built for them.

Meet the founders

Saebyl McDoulett
Founder · Software Builder · BI Engineer
Software Dev Data Engineering Business Intelligence Product Strategy

Saebyl didn't set out to be a software founder. She set out to do something useful with a math degree — and ended up spending nearly a decade in financial services, learning how data actually drives business decisions from the inside.

A layoff and a lucky application later, she landed at Amazon as a Business Intelligence Engineer. It turned out to be exactly the education she needed: what does it look like when data infrastructure is done right, at real scale? And more importantly — why doesn't any of that exist for small businesses?

Finding The Beat LLC is her answer to that question. Every product she builds carries that BI lens — not because data is the point, but because good software is built on understanding what actually matters to the people using it.

How we work

What we actually believe

These aren't values from a brand workshop. They're the things we kept coming back to when we asked ourselves why we were doing this in the first place.

🛠️
Build for the real world
Not the idealized customer. Not the enterprise. The actual small business owner wearing six hats who doesn't have time for a learning curve.
💰
Price like you mean it
Good software shouldn't require a dedicated budget line. If the price makes someone hesitate, the price is wrong. We'd rather have more customers than more margin.
🤝
Your success is personal
We're not a faceless SaaS company. We know what it costs to start something. We genuinely want the businesses using our tools to make it.
🚫
No grow-at-all-costs
We're building a sustainable studio, not chasing an exit. Our goal is to do great work for people we care about and support our family doing it.
📊
Data should work for you
Every product we ship is built with a BI engineer's eye. Not because data is the product — because understanding what matters makes the software better.
🏠
Oklahoma is the point
Our families are there. Our customers are there. We're not building in Oklahoma because we have to — we're going back because that's where we want to be.
